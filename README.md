# Indian Banking Sector Analysis | Wealth Management & Alternate Investments (WMAI)

<sub>

### Overview
This project presents an end-to-end analysis of the **Indian Banking Sector** by combining **Fundamental Analysis** and **Technical Analysis** to evaluate the financial strength, market performance, and investment attractiveness of leading Indian banks.

The study compares **Private Sector Banks** and **Public Sector Banks (PSBs)** to identify sectoral strengths, weaknesses, leaders, laggards, and long-term investment opportunities. The objective is to support informed investment decision-making within the domain of **Wealth Management and Alternate Investments (WMAI)**.

---

### Banks Analyzed

**Private Sector Banks**
- HDFC Bank  
- ICICI Bank  
- Axis Bank  

**Public Sector Banks**
- State Bank of India (SBI)  
- Punjab National Bank (PNB)  
- Bank of India (BOI)  

---

### Project Objectives
- Compare the structural differences between **private and public sector banks** in India.
- Evaluate banking performance using **financial ratios and key performance indicators**.
- Conduct **intra-group and inter-group comparisons** to identify leaders and laggards.
- Perform **technical analysis** using market-based indicators and risk-return measures.
- Generate **Buy / Hold / Sell recommendations** supported by analytical findings.
- Propose portfolio allocation strategies for different investor risk profiles.

---

### Methodology

#### 1. Fundamental Analysis
Assessment of:
- Profitability (ROE, margins)
- Efficiency (cost-to-income ratio)
- Leverage & Solvency
- Valuation metrics
- Growth trends (2021–2025)

#### 2. Technical Analysis
Analysis using:
- CAGR & Volatility
- Maximum Drawdown
- RSI (Relative Strength Index)
- MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
- Moving Average Signals (MA50 vs MA200)
- Correlation & Diversification Analysis
- Risk-adjusted performance measures

---

### Key Insights
- **Private sector banks**, especially **HDFC Bank** and **ICICI Bank**, demonstrated superior profitability, operational efficiency, and sustainable growth.
- **SBI** emerged as a relatively stable public sector bank with balanced risk-adjusted performance.
- **PNB** and **BOI** displayed higher volatility and weaker fundamentals despite turnaround potential.
- Technical indicators highlighted tactical investment opportunities and varying momentum signals across banks.

### Portfolio Recommendations
The study proposes portfolio strategies for different investor categories:

- **Conservative Investors** → Stability-focused allocation with lower volatility exposure.  
- **Balanced Investors** → Combination of growth and stability.  
- **Aggressive Investors** → Higher-risk allocation focused on alpha generation and turnaround opportunities.
