# Indian Banking Sector Analysis
#  Wealth Management & Alternate Investments (WMAI)

## Overview
This project presents an end-to-end analysis of the **Indian Banking Sector** by combining both **Fundamental Analysis** and **Technical Analysis** to evaluate the financial strength, market performance, and investment attractiveness of leading Indian banks.

The study focuses on comparing **Private Sector Banks** and **Public Sector Banks (PSBs)** to identify sectoral strengths, weaknesses, leaders, laggards, and long-term investment opportunities. The analysis aims to support informed decision-making in the context of **Wealth Management and Alternate Investments (WMAI)**.

## Banks Analyzed
### Private Sector Banks
- HDFC Bank  
- ICICI Bank  
- Axis Bank  

### Public Sector Banks
- State Bank of India (SBI)  
- Punjab National Bank (PNB)  
- Bank of India (BOI)  

## Project Objectives
- Compare the structural differences between **private and public sector banks** in India.
- Evaluate banking performance using **financial ratios and key performance indicators**.
- Conduct **intra-group and inter-group comparisons** to identify leaders and laggards.
- Perform **technical analysis** using market-based indicators and risk-return measures.
- Generate **Buy / Hold / Sell recommendations** supported by data-driven insights.
- Propose portfolio allocation strategies for different investor risk profiles.

## Methodology
The project combines:

### 1. Fundamental Analysis
Assessment of:
- Profitability (ROE, margins)
- Efficiency (cost-to-income ratio)
- Leverage & Solvency
- Valuation metrics
- Growth trends (2021–2025)

### 2. Technical Analysis
Analysis using:
- CAGR & Volatility
- Maximum Drawdown
- RSI (Relative Strength Index)
- MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
- Moving Average Signals (MA50 vs MA200)
- Correlation & Diversification Analysis
- Risk-adjusted performance evaluation

## Key Insights
- **Private sector banks**, especially **HDFC Bank** and **ICICI Bank**, demonstrated superior profitability, operational efficiency, and sustainable growth.
- **SBI** emerged as a relatively stable and balanced public sector bank with moderate growth potential.
- **PNB** and **BOI** showed higher volatility and weaker fundamentals despite turnaround potential.
- Technical indicators suggested varying momentum signals across banks, helping identify tactical investment opportunities.
